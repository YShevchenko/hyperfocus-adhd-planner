# Codex Review — HyperFocus (ADHD Planner)

## Product & Documentation Alignment
- HyperFocus is positioned as the “anti-planner” that hides the to-do list, surfaces exactly one micro-step, and keeps an offline “Paralysis Breaker” database plus time-blindness chimes behind a one-time $14.99 unlock (`docs/SPEC.md`). The spec and test plan stress that the heuristics must stay frictionless, the “One Thing Mode” transitions cleanly, and the background audio fires every 5/10/15 minutes even with the device locked (`docs/TEST-PLAN.md`).
- The marketing plan (`docs/MARKETING.md`) classifies the app as a “Tier 1 Cash Cow” for TikTok/Instagram, leaning on the “No ADHD Tax” hook, but the document lacks CPI/CRO goals, spend ceilings, creative cadence, or any instrumentation requirements despite promising to scale globally.

## Code & QA Observations
- `TemplateService` (`lib/domain/services/template_service.dart`) loads `assets/data/templates.json` and hands templated instructions to the heuristics, but there is zero validation of the JSON schema or fallbacks for parsing failures. Since the product spec champions heuristic fallbacks when users create custom tasks, we now have no guard rails: a malformed template or asset change would lead to `TaskTemplate.fromJson` throwing before the UI can show the serene “One Thing Mode.” There are also no unit tests matching the heuristic expectations described in `docs/TEST-PLAN.md`.
- `OneThingScreen` (`lib/presentation/screens/one_thing_screen.dart`) relies on `TimerService` and `ChimeForegroundService` to keep timers and audio alive, but `ChimeForegroundService.start` never checks for Android notification/foreground-permission failures (Android 13+ requires `POST_NOTIFICATIONS`) and silently swallows errors from `flutter_foreground_task`. When the service fails to start, the marketing promise that “Time Blindness Audio fires even when locked” is broken and the user sees no fallback chime because there is no error channel.
- `TimerService` (`lib/domain/services/timer_service.dart`) powers the pie timer and chime stream, yet there are no automated tests ensuring the timer survives pause/resume or that the `progress` getter stays bounded. Without integration coverage for the chime flow, a regression in the service (e.g., `_remainingSeconds` going negative) would slip past the “E2E transitions” section of the test plan.
- The monetization surface mixes ads and the premium flag, but instrumentation is missing. `AdService` (`lib/services/ad_service.dart`) preloads banners/interstitials and has no global consent flow beyond the ATT request on iOS (`lib/services/consent_service.dart`); there is no GDPR/UMP handling for European geos even though marketing targets a global English launch, so any UA campaign touching the EU may violate policy before spend hits $14.99. `IapService` (`lib/services/iap_service.dart`) merely toggles `_isPremium` and exposes a stream, but there are no analytic hooks (Firebase, Amplitude, etc.) to measure the “high impulse buy” conversion, nor does it surface restore failures to the UI, making the “No ADHD Tax” story impossible to prove to finance.
- `TaskNotifier` (`lib/presentation/providers/task_notifier.dart`) writes completions into `SqliteHistoryRepository` and triggers `AdService.trackTaskCompletion`, but both operations are fire-and-forget without error handling. If a database insert fails (disk full) the task silently disappears from history and the UI keeps showing the task as complete even though `history` never received it, which undermines the “build momentum” promise of the spec.

## Marketing & Revenue Risks
- `docs/MARKETING.md` says “global launch” but provides zero geographies, CPI/LTV assumptions, or localization plans, yet the app bundle is English-only and the heuristics copy is hard-coded. This gap makes it hard to scale paid UA because we have no way to test translation or measure whether a given “No ADHD Tax” creative outperforms another.
- There is no creative calendar or spend cadence, so the “high impulse” claim has no guardrails—if the first TikTok creative underperforms, there is nothing in the doc to signal when to pull spend or pivot the hook.
- Because neither `IapService` nor `AdService` emit revenue or conversion events, the marketing team cannot link campaign spend back to the premium conversion rate or prove the “one-time purchase > $50 coaching” claim.

## Next Steps
1. Add deterministic tests for `TemplateService`, `TaskNotifier`, and `TimerService` to cover the heuristic fallback logic, step transitions, and timer persistence described in `docs/TEST-PLAN.md`. Include golden template fixtures so future schema changes fail fast.
2. Harden `ChimeForegroundService` with notification permission checks and error reporting (e.g., fallback to in-app audio when the foreground task fails) so the time-blindness promise actually works on Android 13+ and OEM power-saving locks.
3. Introduce GDPR/UMP consent alongside the existing ATT flow and log impressions/conversions in `AdService` so any global UA spend satisfies policy and we can monitor actual CPM/CPI for “high impulse” geos.
4. Surface purchase/restore errors in `IapService` as user-facing messages and emit analytics events (purchase started, success, failure) so finance can correlate creative lift with revenue and validate the “No ADHD Tax” positioning.
5. Expand `docs/MARKETING.md` with budgets, CPI/LTV targets, creative variants, retargeting cadence, and localization calls-to-action; link those figures to observable telemetry so the cash-cow thesis can be tested instead of being an assumption.
