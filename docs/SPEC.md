# HyperFocus Product Spec

## 1. Product Identity

### 1.1 Working Product Name
HyperFocus (ADHD Paralysis Breaker)

### 1.2 Product Category
Mobile productivity utility for neurodivergent brains (ADHD/Executive Dysfunction).

### 1.3 Product Type
Privacy-first, offline iOS and Android app built with React Native and Expo.

### 1.4 Core Identity Statement
HyperFocus is the anti-planner. Traditional to-do apps induce anxiety by presenting users with a wall of text they are already avoiding. HyperFocus acknowledges Executive Dysfunction and "ADHD Paralysis." It hides everything except the *one next micro-step*. It breaks down overwhelming tasks into absurdly small, frictionless actions. It is a one-time purchase that requires no subscriptions and keeps all data completely private.

## 2. Vision Statement

Productivity apps are built for neurotypical brains that can sequence complex tasks. The vision for HyperFocus is to be the "Emergency Button for Burnout." When a user stares at a messy room and cannot start, the app asks: "Are there any plates? Take 1 plate to the sink." It provides dopamine hits for tiny actions, building momentum until the task is done.

## 3. Core Concept

The user opens the app and is greeted by a single large question: "What's overwhelming you right now?"
They type or select an area (e.g., "Cleaning the Kitchen").

The app activates **One Thing Mode**:
1. The screen goes black except for a single large, friendly instruction: "1. Grab a trash bag."
2. A large "Done" button sits at the bottom.
3. A visual time-blindness timer (a pie filling up) slowly ticks down from 2 minutes.
4. When tapped, the screen clears, a satisfying haptic/sound plays, and the next step appears: "2. Throw away any visible trash."

If the user enters a custom task (e.g., "Write Essay"), the app uses a local heuristic engine to apply a generic "Getting Started" framework: "1. Open the document." -> "2. Write one terrible sentence." -> "3. Set a 10-minute focus timer."

## 4. Target Audience
- Adults diagnosed with ADHD or executive dysfunction.
- People experiencing burnout or severe stress.
- Anyone who feels "paralyzed" when looking at a long list of chores.

## 5. Monetization Strategy

**Freemium (One-Time IAP)**
- **Free Tier:** The core "One Thing Mode" where users manually enter their own micro-steps, plus a visual focus timer.
- **Premium Tier ($14.99 One-Time):** Unlocks the "Paralysis Breaker" database—a local engine containing 300+ pre-broken-down overwhelming tasks (cleaning, organizing, packing, self-care routines). It also unlocks "Time Blindness Audios" (spoken voice announcing the time every 10 mins).
- **Why this maximizes revenue:** The $15 upfront price point is positioned against $50/mo ADHD coaching apps. Neurodivergent users often suffer from the "ADHD Tax" of forgetting to cancel subscriptions. We explicitly market this as "No Subscription. No ADHD Tax." This builds massive goodwill and drives impulse buys.

## 6. Marketing & User Acquisition Strategy
**Classification:** Tier 1 Cash Cow (High Impulse, Low CPI)
**Primary Ad Platforms:** TikTok & Instagram Reels (UGC focused).
**Targeting:** Broad targeting leaning on algorithm. #ADHD, #ExecutiveDysfunction.
**Core Hook:** "Are you paralyzed by a messy room? This anti-planner gives you just ONE absurdly simple step. No $50/mo coaching. No ADHD Tax. Paid once, yours forever."
**Geographic Strategy:** Global English launch. Maintain $14.99 price point to position against expensive subscription coaches.
